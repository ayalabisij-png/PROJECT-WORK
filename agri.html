Agri-Business & Agri-Tech

Agriculture is the backbone of Uganda's economy. By combining traditional farming with modern technology and business strategies, the next generation is transforming how we grow, sell, and export food.

Why this field matters

With over 70% of Uganda's working population employed in agriculture, there is massive potential for innovation. Agri-Tech (Agricultural Technology) introduces tools like mobile apps for weather forecasting, digital market platforms, and precision farming techniques to increase yields and reduce waste.

Agri-Business focuses on the commercial side: turning farms into profitable enterprises, managing supply chains, adding value to raw goods (like turning fruit into juice for export), and navigating international trade.

Fast Facts

  • Growth Potential: High. Integration of mobile money and smartphones is opening new markets for rural farmers.
  • Impact: Crucial for food security and climate change resilience in East Africa.
  • Work Environment: Ranges from corporate offices in Kampala to field-work in rural districts.

Specific Career Roles

Agri-Tech Software Developer

Creates apps and USSD platforms that help farmers track weather, monitor soil health, and connect directly with buyers without middlemen.

Agribusiness Manager

Oversees the business operations of large farms or cooperatives. They handle budgeting, marketing, supply chain logistics, and exporting goods.

Value Addition Specialist

Focuses on processing raw agricultural products into finished goods (e.g., turning raw coffee beans into packaged roasted coffee) to increase their market value.

How to get started

Education & Degrees

  • • BSc in Agriculture or Agribusiness Management
  • • BSc in Computer Science / IT (for Agri-Tech)
  • • Diplomas in Crop/Animal Husbandry
  • • Food Science and Technology degrees

Valuable Skills to Learn

  • • Data analysis and basic coding (Python, mobile dev)
  • • Entrepreneurship and financial literacy
  • • Understanding of climate-smart agriculture
  • • Marketing and supply chain management
